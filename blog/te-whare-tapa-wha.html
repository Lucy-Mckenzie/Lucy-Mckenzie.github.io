Te Whare Tapa Whā

- Te Whare Tapa Whā is a Māori model of health and well-being that highlights the importance of balance across four pillars: taha tinana (physical well-being), taha hinengaro (mental/emotional well-being), taha whānau (social well-being), and taha wairua (spiritual well-being).

Wall Activities
Taha Tinana (Physical Wellbeing)1. I run around 25km weekly for fitness and mental clarity, usually before breakfast/after dinner.
2. I also practice yoga and breathing exercises weekly for focus and peace.
3. Every second day some members of my family and I go swimming at the beach.
Taha Whānau (Family Wellbeing)1. My family and I eat dinner every night at the table chatting about the day.
2. Mum and I go for walks together most days.
Taha Hinengaro (Emotional and Mental Wellbeing)1. I wake up early and read for while/ meditate or reflect on my dreams. My favourite time of day.
Taha Wairua (Spiritual Wellbeing)1. I have a collage book that I write and print a lot of manifests in, this book is very sacred to me.
2. Yoga with my mum and my little sister is also very special.
Whenua (Interconnections to the land and environment)1. I make sure to get outside first thing in the morning and get some daylight.
2. I'm an avid cloud and tree spotter, I'm often caught looking over through the trees and clouds identifying and determining the weather for the day.
